Code #1

Observations

Looks like hex but ranges are off for Ascii. If you are thinking that it’s colours that would make only 8 words of 6 bits, not enough for a passcode.

Hint
nzbyv rg’h zgyzhs
Solution

Let’s try making pairs

b0 aa ad b4 ac 94 93 a8 aa ba 98 88 a9 ba 93 b0 a9 ba 90 ca ad 85 ad ac

Interesting to see that many pairs start with A and B. In the past “hex atbash” as been used several times and A and B convert to 5 and 4 which makes a good Ascii range.

Here’s the index for the “hex atbash”:

0123456789abcdef
fedcba9876543210

Atbash all pairs

b0 aa ad b4 ac 94 93 a8 aa ba 98 88 a9 ba 93 b0 a9 ba 90 ca ad 85 ad ac

4f 55 52 4b 53 6b 6c 57 55 45 67 77 56 45 6c 4f 56 45 6f 35 52 7a 52 53

Convert hex to Ascii

OURKSklWUEgwVElOVEo5RzRS

Not quite there yet but that does look like Base64, convert it to text

9DJJIVPH0TINTJ9G4R

Change that IV to 4 and that 0 to O

9DJJ4PHOTINTJ9G4R

Code #3

Observations

All symbols, usually means to convert to numbers

Hint
~ is used to split the groups

Solution

Since ~ does not convert to a number on a keyboard, let assume it is used to split the groups.

$% !%!% !& &! #^ & !%@ @ !& # %!^ & !^! #^ !& @& !^

Now let’s convert those symbols to their respective keyboard numbers

45 1515 17 71 36 7 152 2 17 3 516 7 161 36 17 27 16

Notice how number from 1 to 4 are never beside each other and same thing for 5 to 8 ? That’s a hint that we are searching for a cipher that only uses 2 bits. Morse is one of them, here’s the index:

1=-
2=--
3=---
4=----
5=.
6=..
7=...
8=....

Now let’s translate the number sequence to morse

----. -.-. -... ...- ---.. ... -.-- -- -... --- .-.. ... -..- ---.. -... --... -..

Convert morse to text
