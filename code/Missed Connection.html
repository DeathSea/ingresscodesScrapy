Code #1

Observations

The passcode format is right there, go through your usual cipher routine.

Solution

Atbash and ROT didn’t give anything, next step is morse.

.-.. ..- -.-. ..--- ---.. -- -. --- -..- -- -.- - --... ...-- ..--- -. ..

You’ll need to reverse and swap the morse code to get the right passcode

-- -. ...-- ..--- ---.. . .-. .. .--. ... -. .. --... ...-- -.-. .-- --.-

Convert morse to text

MN328ERIPSNI73CWQ

Reverse

QWC37INSPIRE823NM

Code #2

Observations

All letters, no particular range.

Hint
Nop not morse this time, more like numbers
Solution

My first instinct was to see if morse would give a pattern but nothing came out. Let’s try converting letters to numbers (a=0)

23 21 4 25 4 5 4 3 3 3 5 3 13 0 2 6 4 13 25 7 5 6 5 6 23 4 3

Now let’s pair them up.

23 21 42 54 54 33 35 31 30 26 41 32 57 56 56 23 43

A few 5 in there could mean Ascii numbers but the rest is not in the right range, should be in the 6, 7 & 8 range. Notice how they are in the 2, 3 & 4 range? Let’s atbash those numbers (1=9 and 0=0)

87 89 68 56 56 77 75 79 70 84 69 78 53 54 54 87 67

Convert decimal to Ascii

WYD88MKOFTEN566WC

Code #3

Observations

All the right ingredients, that same old cipher again.

Solution

Make a 4×3 grid

m 3 m 3
b 7 9 c
n x 8 k

Read down from the upper left M

mbn37xm983ck

Invalid ? Reverse the second row

m 3 m 3
c 9 7 b
n x 8 k

Read down from the upper left M

mcn39xm783bk
