Two codes could be found in the HTML source of the In need of a friend… post on the Investigate Ingress blog.

[1] tam61/468cw

[2] uKJeLG8-M5I.77V77G86X52$54#52@54#50^67R69D

Code #1

Observations

Passcode format is there. A simple manipulation and a keyword sub will do it.

Solution

Atbash the letters and numbers

gzn49/642xd

/ is glyph for attack

gzn49attack642xd

Invalid, the reverse of attack is retreat

gzn49retreat642xd

Code #2

Observations

Fun one, there is a split point that will lead you to the keywords and the rest to the fixes.

Solution

Having a closer look at the string past the dot you can see that there are 10 numbers in Ascii range and another 10 characters made up of letters and symbols.

Let’s split at the dot

uKJeLG8-M5I
77V77G86X52$54#52@54#50^67R69D

Using the second string, separate the numbers then the letters and symbols

77 77 86 52 54 52 54 50 67 69

VGX$#@#^RD

Convert decimal to Ascii and symbols to their respective numbers on the keyboard

MMV46462CE

VGX43236RD

Searching uKJeLG8-M5I on Google gives a video name Ghostwriter. Ghost and Write are valid keywords

mmv46ghost462ce

vgx43write236rd
