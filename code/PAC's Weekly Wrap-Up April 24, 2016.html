3 codes where hidden in the PAC’s Weekly Wrap-Up: April 24, 2016 post on the Investigate Ingress blog.

[1] 2n7vncer8cye8r

[2] 2aj4vukof7ij6da

[3] j*c%d&erif$abb^

Code #1

Observations

Right amount of numbers, total of 14 characters which means we are looking for a 4 letter keyword. You can almost make it out in that middle section.

Solution

Split into pairs

2n 7v nc er 8c ye 8r

Reverse each pairs

n2 v7 cn re c8 ey r8

n2v7cnrec8eyr8

Reverse

8rye8cernc7v2n

Code #2

Observations

Again, all the ingredients are there for a code, just need to line them up properly

Solution

Make a 3×5 rectangle

2 a j
4 v u
k o f
7 i j
6 d a

You can now see the keyword showing up in the middle column. Prefix and suffix need a bit of work.

Reverse rows 2 and 4

2 a j
u v 4
k o f
j i 7
6 d a

Read down from 2

2ukj6avoidj4f7a

Code #3

Observations

Symbols have been used in the past, look carefully and you can see the passcode format.

Solution

reverse the string

^bba$fire&d%c*j

All numbers on the QWERTY keyboard have symbols in the shift position. Convert those symbols to numbers.

6bba4fire7d5c8j

Now we need a sub for fire7. Spent a bit of time Googling it but couldn’t get a reference to any keyword. Then I thought that & shouldn’t be converted to a number which would make ‘Fire &’ or ‘Fire And …’. Fire and Ice came to mind but that’s not a keyword. I then remembered the days where I played Falcon 4.0 on my old Atari computer. They had a missile class called Fire and Forget.

6bba4forgetd5c8j
