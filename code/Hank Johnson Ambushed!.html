Three codes were found in the Hank Johnson: Ambushed! post on the Investigate Ingress blog.

[1] mdfbnZy3re5bmteWsdqYuunA

[2] mxtehdfabfdhfegh

[3] x2e7sfp55s5t

Code #1

Observations

24 characters made of capital and lowercase letters and numbers. You should recognize that cipher.

Solution

Converting Base64 to text doesn’t give anything. Let’s try changing the case of the letters

MDFBNzY3RE5BMTEwSDQyUUNa

Convert Base64 to text

01A767DNA110H42QCZ

You can see that the passcode format is reversed. Reverse the string

ZCQ24H011AND767A10

Convert 011 to oll from leetspeak and 10 to x from roman numeral

zcq24holland767ax

Code #2

Observations

All letters without any particular range… except for that middle part

Solution

Let’s split in the passcode format to see what we are working with

mxt eh dfabfd hfe gh

If you look at where numbers position should be and the keyword, the letters are all in the A to J range.

Converts these letters to numbers

mxt 47 350153 754 gh

For the keyword part, convert from leetspeak and roman numerals

mxt47evolve754gh

Code #3

Observations

12 characters with just the right ingredients for a passcode format

Solution

Make a 2×6 grid

x 2 e 7 s f
p 5 5 s 5 t

Read going down from the upper right F

fts57se525xp
