Inside the post Acolyte’s Compound Attacked, over at the Investigate Ingress blog, there were three codes hidden.

[1] 584332215336454335344532421059205643
[2] wsrrk5r8oes2k6sgpj
[3] qrfrvrifveohujghxbug

Code #1

Observations

All numbers which can be paired up, most likely a conversion to ASCII.

Hint
When the values at the number positions are higher than those for the alphas, we should…?
Solution
Pairing the numbers gives 18 pairs
58 43 32 21 53 36 45 43 35 34 45 32 42 10 59 20 56 43
Since the values in the number positions are higher than the others, we need to “invert” them, meaning numerical Atbash (123456789 = 987654321)
52 67 78 89 57 74 65 67 75 76 65 78 68 90 51 80 54 67
Convert Decimal to ASCII
4CNY9JACKLANDZ3P6C

Code #2

Observations

There are the right amount of numbers and it looks like there can be a keyword hidden in there, just needs a bit of shuffling.

Solution

Form the code into a 6×3 grid
w s r r k 5
r 8 o e s 2
k 6 s g p j

Reverse the middle row

w s r r k 5
2 s e o 8 r
k 6 s g p j

Read from the top right (5) down each row
5rjk8progresss6w2k

Code #3

Observations

No numbers but length is about right for written out numbers.

Hint
Remember the first steps to try.
Solution
Apply the Caesarian ROT 13
deseievsirbuhwtukoht
Now the numbers seem to appear and a keyword also, lets reverse the string
thokutwhubrisveiesed
Substitute the numbers
3oku2hubrisv8e7d
