Code #1

Observations

18 characters with the right amount of numbers to get a passcode

Solution

Make a 6×3 grid

p 5 a b l 2
o 2 e m 7 r
w c p l 5 w

You can almost make out the keyword in the middle. Reverse the middle row

p 5 a b l 2
r 7 m e 2 o
w c p l 5 w

Read going down from the upper left P

prw57campbell252ow

Code #2

Observations

20 characters. Look carefully at the start of the string

Solution

Rot, Atbash, Morse, Rectangle and a few other ciphers didn’t give anything. We have 20 characters and we know that we need at least 10 for prefix and suffix. Let’s split the string in two

nxzehgfgfe
kfhmtgltzw

On the first string, aside from the 3 letters at the start, they could all be converted to numbers. Let’s assume this is the prefix and suffix, we would get

nxz47656fe

Now we need to find the keyword that is hidden in the second string. Let’s try vigenere.

Passphrase: nxzehgfgfe

Message: kfhmtgltzw

Result: xiiimagnus

nxz4713magnus656fe
