Hidden in the Hank Johnson: What caused the XM drought? blog post, we can find these 3 codes:

[1] 43245322133333243315131135444542151521245115123324331555
[2] cyshpor3ectk49p5
[3] 4p59decb8orowboq

Code #1

Observations

There is an even number of digits, all in the range from 1 to 5.

Solution

Split the code into pairs of digits:
43 24 53 22 13 33 33 24 33 15 13 11 35 44 45 42 15 15 21 24 51 15 12 33 24 33 15 55

Translate the pairs to letters using a Polybius Square, then substitute the words for the respective digits to make it a passcode:

sixgcnninecaptureefivebninez
6gcn9capturee5b9z

Codes #2 and #3

Observations

Both codes are of equal length and are mirroring each other’s digit/letter structure.

Solution

Arrange both as 4×4 rectangles.

4p59
decb
8oro
wboq

cysh
por3
ectk
49p5

Now the required format can be seen better, read out the  first code column-wise down and from right to left, the second one column-wise up and from left to right:

9boq5cropeob4d8w
4epc9coyptrs5k3h

Which, unfortunately, doesn’t make any sense.

But if we pick digits and letters alternating between the codes:

4bpq9cooperb5d3w
9eoc5cryptos4k8h
